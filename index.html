Concentration in Virtual Reality (VR), Augmented Reality (AR), Robotics, Computer Vision, and Machine Learning

  • Current Ventures

    I am a Computer Science PhD graduate from the University of Central Florida. I gratudated under the supervision of of my advisors, Dr. Annie Wu andDr. Ryan McMahan. I completed my dissertation defense under my committee members Dr. Annie Wu, Dr. Ryan McMahan, Dr. Lori Walters, and Dr. Charlie Hughes. My dissertation work focused on examining the systematic evaluation of performance of SLAM under different synthetic environments. The key goal of my research was to determine specific environment factors that can affect the performance of SLAM algorithms. My research has spanned multiple fields such as computer vision, robotics, and XR development but has primiarily focused on robot vision for my dissertation.

    I have multiple academic projects over the years. These have included the CLOVR data collection tool for SteamVR applications, RecolorCloud point cloud editor plugin suite, FinalCloud point cloud editing and recolorer tool, Mixed reality project - Gesture recording studio, Neural network for comparing genetic algorithms in imputed data, Immersive final demo for VR class, and multiple VR and AR projects during my undergraduate and graduate career. During my undergraduate career I also worked on robotics projects such as developing drivers for haptic devices (Manus VR 2018), implementing the MS Hololens for autonomous behavior for a Pioneer robot (2017 - 2018), and an Android app for collecting user input and recording average densities for UI presses (2019).

    Additionally I have done final projects with computer vision and machine learning applications such as developing a notebook script forking DeepGRU by Mehran Maghoumi to locate similar strands of genomes through tokenizing individual chromosomes and determining similar patterns (2021).
